# N5 Unit Map (35 units)

All 35 units of the N5 ladder, sequenced from first words to can-do checkpoint. Words marked
[existing] are already in `src/content/vocabulary/`; all others need to be added. Phrases need
sourcing from the existing phrase corpus or Tatoeba before they go into SRS content.

Template recap: **5–7 words · 1–2 kanji · 1 grammar pattern · 2–3 phrases · produce beat**

---

## Situation 1 — Greetings & basics (Units 1–7)
*Can-dos: #1 greet someone, #2 introduce yourself, #3 thank someone, #4 say you don't understand*

---

### Unit 1 — Yes, no, and this/that

**Builds toward:** can-do #1

**Words:** はい (yes), いいえ (no), これ [existing], それ [existing], あれ [existing]
**Kanji:** 日 — にち/ひ, sun / day (appears in 今日, 日本)
**Grammar:** ～は～です — X is Y (the fundamental copula)
**Phrases:**
- これは本です — This is a book
- それは何ですか — What is that?
**Produce beat:** Hana holds up an object — tap the right word (これ / それ / あれ)

---

### Unit 2 — Hello & goodbye

**Builds toward:** can-do #1

**Words:** おはようございます (good morning), こんにちは (hello), こんばんは (good evening),
  さようなら (goodbye), またね (see you later)
**Kanji:** (none — all kana; unit is short to front-load wins)
**Grammar:** ～ですね — confirmation / softening ("It is, isn't it?")
**Phrases:**
- おはようございます — Good morning
- こんにちは、おげんきですか — Hello, how are you?
**Produce beat:** Hana greets you at different times of day — respond with the right greeting.

---

### Unit 3 — Nice to meet you

**Builds toward:** can-do #2 (introduce yourself)

**Words:** はじめまして (nice to meet you), よろしくおねがいします (pleased to meet you),
  なまえ (name), わたし [existing], あなた [existing]
**Kanji:** 名 — めい/な, name
**Grammar:** わたしは～です — I am ~ (self-introduction pattern)
**Phrases:**
- はじめまして、わたしは[name]です — Nice to meet you, I'm [name]
- よろしくおねがいします — Pleased to meet you
**Produce beat:** Hana introduces herself — introduce yourself back with your name.

---

### Unit 4 — Where are you from?

**Builds toward:** can-do #2

**Words:** くに (country), にほん (Japan), えいご (English), から (from), どこ [existing]
**Kanji:** 国 — こく/くに, country; 本 — ほん/もと, origin/book
**Grammar:** ～から来ました — I came from ~ (origin pattern)
**Phrases:**
- どこから来ましたか — Where are you from?
- アメリカから来ました — I'm from America
**Produce beat:** Hana asks where you're from — answer with your country.

---

### Unit 5 — Thank you & you're welcome

**Builds toward:** can-do #3 (thank someone)

**Words:** ありがとうございます (thank you), すみません (excuse me / thank you),
  どういたしまして (you're welcome), ごめんなさい (I'm sorry), いいえ (no / not at all)
**Kanji:** (none)
**Grammar:** ありがとうございます / どういたしまして — the thanks exchange
**Phrases:**
- ありがとうございます — Thank you very much
- いいえ、どういたしまして — No, not at all (you're welcome)
**Produce beat:** Hana does something helpful — thank her; she responds, you reply.

---

### Unit 6 — I don't understand

**Builds toward:** can-do #4 (say you don't understand)

**Words:** わかりません (I don't understand), もういちど (one more time),
  ゆっくり (slowly), おねがいします (please), わかる [existing]
**Kanji:** (none)
**Grammar:** ～をおねがいします — please do ~ (polite request)
**Phrases:**
- すみません、わかりません — Excuse me, I don't understand
- もういちど、ゆっくりおねがいします — One more time, slowly please
**Produce beat:** Hana says something fast — use the right phrase to ask her to slow down.

---

### Unit 7 — Numbers 1–10

**Builds toward:** can-dos #9, #11 (prices, tickets)

**Words:** いち、に、さん、よん/し、ご、ろく、なな/しち、はち、きゅう/く、じゅう
**Kanji:** 一、二、三、四、五
**Grammar:** ～つ — counting small things (ひとつ, ふたつ, みっつ...)
**Phrases:**
- ひとつください — One please
- みっつあります — There are three
**Produce beat:** Hana shows items — say how many using ～つ.

### Unit 12 — Bigger numbers (hundreds & thousands)
